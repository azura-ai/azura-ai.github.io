# Azura AI Outreach Playbook (Scrappy Edition)

Using a personal email (`kinza99.rj@gmail.com`) for outreach requires a **"High-Touch, Expert Practitioner"** approach. We handle this by being incredibly specific and consultative. 

### 🏹 The "Efficiency Gap" Lead Signal
When you see a job posting for "Data Entry," "Manual Processing" or "Clerical Support," do not apply for the job. Instead, contact the hiring manager or department head with this pitch.

---

### 📧 Outreach Template 1: The "Hiring Helper" (Best for Email/LinkedIn)
**Subject**: [Company Name] — Automating the [Job Title] bottleneck

> "Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed you are currently hiring for a [Job Title] role. I’ve helped European firms in [Industry] replace high-volume manual work with autonomous AI workflows. 
> 
> Specifically, I build systems that handle [mention task: e.g., invoice extraction, claims processing, document audit] with 99% accuracy at a fraction of the cost of a full-time hire.
>
> I’m not looking for a job; I’m interested in helping you solve the efficiency gap that prompted the hire in the first place. 
> 
> Can I send you a 2-minute 'Automation Audit' for [Company Name] showing how we could handle this workload with AI?
>
> Best,
> Kinza"

---

### 🛡️ Why This Works:
1.  **Low Friction**: You aren't "selling" a service; you're offering to "help solve the gap."
2.  **Specific ROI**: You're mentioning 99% accuracy and cost reduction.
3.  **The 'Gmail' Flip**: Using a personal email sounds like an **Independent Technical Expert** rather than a "spammy agency." This builds more initial trust for a technical audit.
